Barber Glossary

Refer to these terms to help you to communicate and deliver your idea better with your barber.

Taper

Means shorter hair around the perimeter of the haircut gradually getting longer. Sometimes it makes more sense to think of it as longer hair tapered down to shorter hair.

Skin Taper / Skin Fade

Means that the sides and/or back are shaved to the skin and the top is blended or faded into slightly longer hair.

Disconnected

A dramatic length changes in the hair and creates a noticeable contrast between the sides of the hair and the top.

Clipper / Kliper

An electric device with vibrating or rotating blades used for shaving or cutting hair close to the skin.

Capes / Kip

A fabric to cover your body from stray hairs.

Pomade

There are two major types of pomade: petroleum based and water based. Petroleum based promades are the original kind. They are made with real grease and fatty ingredients to produce the shine and hold. Water based pomades are water soluble making them easier to wash out of the hair than actual grease. Pomades are meant for those that want to achieve slick, neat and shiny and especially for styles like the pompadour, quiff or ducktail. Unlike gels,this product does not dry out and will not harden on the hair.

Wax

Wax, pastes and clays are one in the same. These are significantly of a thicker consistency than other styling products but again unlike gel will not stiffen in the hair although will feel "tackier" than most products. Some waxes add a little shine but most are matte finish which is great for the messy, dry look with a strong hold.

Hair Gel

Translucent in color and generally thinner in consistency than promades or waxes but can be considered "lightweight" or "heavyweight" depending on hold. Unlike waxes and pomades, gels actually harden on the hair leaving a stiff feeling and a shiny, wet look. For those with thin or thinning hair it is recommended to use a lightweight gel and opposite for those with thick or curly/wavy hair.

Hair Spray

Hair sprays can be used on their own to add volume or boost the roots and also can be added after your favourite product for additional shine and an extra kick of hold.
